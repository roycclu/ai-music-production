# Production Brief: The Good Still Burning
*A major | 94 BPM | 4/4*

## Production Style
A slow-burning Americana-soul ballad fused with progressive-electronic architecture — the song lives in the liminal space between a Jason Isbell front-porch fingerpick and an Avicii festival sunrise moment. The acoustic core is never betrayed: Travis-picked guitar, natural room tone, and bluegrass vocal grain remain the emotional spine throughout. But the electronic elements — warm analog synth pads, a four-on-the-floor kick that enters like a heartbeat becoming conscious of itself, shimmering sidechain compression on the chorus pad, and a string arrangement that swells like golden hour light spreading across a field — lift the song from intimate to anthemic without ever feeling synthetic. The production philosophy is 'campfire to cathedral': every element that enters serves the emotional crescendo, and every element that exits serves the vulnerability. The mix should feel like you're sitting three feet from the singer in Verse 1 and standing in an open field with your arms out by Chorus 2. The final whispered line should feel like the field is empty and the stars are out and someone is walking away still humming.

## Instrumentation

- acoustic guitar — Martin D-28 or similar dreadnought, fingerpicked Travis-style in verses, open folk strum in choruses, capo 2 playing G shapes
- banjo — open-back clawhammer, enters Pre-Chorus 1 with sparse melodic fills, rolls gently under Chorus 1, fuller arpeggiated rolls in Chorus 2, absent in Bridge, returns in Outro with single tremolo notes
- kick drum — deep, warm, analog-style 808-meets-Nashville kick, pillowy low end around 55-65 Hz, enters as a subtle pulse in Verse 2 (quarter notes), becomes a confident four-on-the-floor in Chorus 2, sidechains the synth pad for that Avicii pump-and-breathe feel
- snare/clap — layered organic snare (brush hit) with a soft electronic clap, enters Pre-Chorus 1 on beats 2 and 4, opens to a fuller crack in Chorus 2
- hi-hat — programmed but humanized closed hat, 8th notes beginning in Pre-Chorus 1, opens slightly on the and-of-4 in choruses for swing, removed in Bridge
- synth pad — warm analog polysynth (Juno-106 or Prophet-style), long-release A-major chord wash, enters as a barely-perceptible bed in Pre-Chorus 1, swells to full harmonic cushion in Chorus 2, filtered sweep rising through pre-choruses for the Avicii-style anticipation build, sidechain-compressed to kick in choruses for rhythmic breathing
- sub-bass — sine-wave sub following root notes, enters Verse 2 mirroring the guitar bass walk (A-G#-F#-D), becomes a foundational low-end anchor in choruses, rolled off below 30 Hz to keep headroom clean
- bass guitar — warm fingerstyle electric bass (P-bass with flatwounds), plays the descending walk A-G#-F#-D in Verse 2, locks with kick in choruses on root-fifth patterns, drops out entirely in Bridge
- string section — arranged for viola, cello, and two violins; enters Chorus 1 with sustained whole-note pads on chord tones, expands to a counter-melody line (violin 1) in Chorus 2 that traces the vocal melody a third above during 'the years will ache to hold,' full ensemble crescendo on final 'burning gold' of Chorus 2, single sustained cello note (A2) under Bridge's 'amen,' gentle pizzicato arpeggios in Outro fading to nothing
- pedal steel guitar — subtle, weeping fills between vocal phrases in Verse 2 and Chorus 2, adds Americana authenticity and emotional ache, mixed low and wet with plate reverb
- tambourine — light shaker-style tambourine on 8th notes, enters Chorus 1 only, fuller in Chorus 2, hand-played feel with human inconsistency
- atmospheric textures — field recording of summer evening (crickets, distant screen door, faint wind) layered at -30dB under Intro and Verse 1, fades by Pre-Chorus; vinyl crackle texture at -35dB under Bridge for intimacy; single reverse cymbal swell into each chorus entry

## Dynamic Arc

**Intro_4Bars:** Intimate and luminous — solo acoustic guitar fingerpicked Travis-style, letting each note ring into natural room reverb. The descending bass line A→G#→F# through the first three chords establishes the emotional gravity immediately. A barely audible summer-evening field recording (crickets, warm air) sits beneath at threshold volume, placing the listener on the porch. A single warm synth pad tone fades in on bar 3 at the edge of perception — you feel it more than hear it, like heat rising off pavement. Dynamic level: pp to p. The guitar should sound close-mic'd with a large-diaphragm condenser (U87 or similar) about 10 inches from the 12th fret, capturing finger noise and string transients.
**Verse1_16Bars:** Conversational and close — the voice enters low and husky in the A2-D4 range, sitting right on top of the fingerpicked guitar with almost no separation. The vocal should feel like someone talking to you from across a small table. Guitar continues Travis-pattern fingerpicking with thumb alternating bass on A and E strings, index and middle pulling melody on B and high-E strings. No other instruments yet — this is a two-element mix (guitar + voice). Let the bass walk A→G#→F#→D do all the harmonic storytelling. Room tone and field recording texture continue at subliminal levels. The second 8-bar pass can add the faintest whisper of the synth pad on the Dsus2 chord — a color, not a statement. Dynamic level: mp. The vocal mic should capture every grain, every breath, every consonant click. No compression on the vocal bus yet — let the natural dynamics live.
**Prechorus1_8Bars:** The first expansion — guitar transitions from fingerpick to light palm-muted strumming on the F#m7, giving a rhythmic pulse that signals forward motion. Closed hi-hat enters on bar 1 at low velocity, playing straight 8th notes with a slight human swing (58% swing ratio). Snare brush hit on beats 2 and 4, ghosted and barely there. Banjo enters with sparse two-note melodic fills in the gaps between vocal phrases — not competing, just color. The synth pad begins a slow low-pass filter sweep from 800 Hz upward, creating the Avicii-style anticipation build — the listener can feel something coming without knowing what. Sub-bass enters as a gentle sine wave on root notes, felt in the chest more than heard in the ears. The vocal pushes forward in placement and volume — more mf, more open vowels, the singer leaning into the mic. A single reverse cymbal swell begins on bar 7 and crescendos into the chorus downbeat. Dynamic level: mf, building steadily. This section is about controlled anticipation — everything is pointing toward release.
**Chorus1_16Bars:** The bloom — but deliberately holding 20% in reserve. On the downbeat of bar 1, the kick drum enters for the first time (quarter notes, not four-on-the-floor yet — a heartbeat, not a dance), the guitar opens to full folk strumming, and the synth pad filter sweeps fully open to reveal the warm A-major wash, sidechain-compressed to the kick so it breathes and pumps. The banjo plays gentle clawhammer rolls underneath. Strings enter on bar 1 with sustained whole notes on chord tones — the arrangement is restrained, mostly viola and cello providing warmth beneath the vocal. Harmonies enter on bar 5 ('Let the reel keep turning') — a low third (F#3, warm alto timbre) and a high fifth (C#4-E4, airy tenor) flanking the lead. By bar 9 ('Let the porch light flicker') all three vocal parts ring together. Tambourine enters with light 8th notes. The vocal opens into the Aloe Blacc soulful zone — rounder, fuller, chest-forward, with earned vibrato blooming on 'burning' and 'gold.' On 'And the good's still burning gold' (bars 13-14), the strings swell slightly and the three-part harmony locks into a ringing major chord. Bars 15-16 (final | A | A |) are a two-bar instrumental exhale — kick drops to half notes, pad sustains, guitar rings open, allowing the resonance to decay naturally before Verse 2. Dynamic level: f (warm forte), but the mix retains air and space — nothing is pushed to the limiter. The sidechain pump on the pad gives the chorus its Avicii-esque rhythmic identity without ever feeling like EDM — it's a heartbeat, not a club.
**Verse2_16Bars:** Return to intimacy but with earned depth — the guitar returns to fingerpicking, but now with a more intentional bass walk (thumb walking A→G#→F#→D more deliberately, the low notes ringing longer). The kick drum remains as a subtle quarter-note pulse at reduced velocity — it entered the song in Chorus 1 and now lives in the listener's subconscious. Sub-bass follows the guitar's bass walk, doubling the descending line an octave below at low volume. Electric bass guitar enters for the first time, playing the same walking pattern with warm P-bass tone. The vocal returns to mp but is warmer and more resonant than Verse 1 — the chest has opened, the singer has settled deeper into the story. Pedal steel guitar enters with subtle weeping fills between phrases, mixed low and drenched in plate reverb, adding Americana ache. The synth pad retreats to a barely-there bed, filtered back down. Hi-hat continues at reduced velocity. On 'I wanna press my thumb into the face of the clock,' the vocal pushes slightly forward in intensity — this is the lyric where the singer's desire becomes urgent. Dynamic level: mp to mf, with the instrumentation creating a richer bed than Verse 1 even at similar volume.
**Prechorus2_8Bars:** Intensified anticipation — same structure as Pre-Chorus 1 but everything is hotter. Hi-hat opens slightly on the and-of-4. Snare hits are more present (actual rimshot rather than brush). The synth pad filter sweep is faster and reaches higher, with a secondary high-frequency shimmer layer added. Banjo fills are more continuous — 16th-note roll fragments. Strings enter early with a rising counter-line. The kick pushes to steady quarter notes. The vocal is at mf+ with more open vowels and aggressive forward placement — you can hear the singer's commitment increasing. The reverse cymbal swell into Chorus 2 is longer (starting bar 5) and louder, building unmistakable anticipation. A subtle white-noise riser (classic Avicii build technique) sweeps upward underneath everything from bar 5 to bar 8, filtered and mixed at -18dB so it adds tension without screaming 'EDM.' Dynamic level: mf to mf+, all vectors pointing upward.
**Chorus2_16Bars:** The full flowering — this is the emotional and sonic peak of the entire song, the moment where bluegrass authenticity and Avicii euphoria become indistinguishable. The kick opens to a confident four-on-the-floor pattern. The sidechain compression on the synth pad is more aggressive — the pump-and-breathe is now audible and driving. Guitar strumming is full and open, every string ringing. Banjo plays full clawhammer rolls. Bass guitar locks with the kick on a driving root-fifth pattern. Strings expand to full quartet writing: violins play a soaring counter-melody that traces the vocal melody a third above on 'We're alive inside a moment that the years will ache to hold,' viola and cello provide harmonic foundation with eighth-note movement. Pedal steel adds sustained weeping tones on chord changes. Tambourine is fuller. Harmonies are present from bar 1 and are stacked wider and stronger — the high harmony occasionally drifts to a sixth on 'moment that the years will ache to hold' for emotional color. The lead vocal hits its full ff peak — the voice opens completely into the F#4 range, vibrato widens naturally, the Appalachian grain and the soulful roundness fuse completely. On 'And the good's still burning gold' (bars 13-14), every instrument hits its peak: strings crescendo, kick is driving, pad is pumping, three-part harmony rings in a shimmering A-major triad, and the guitar strums with full conviction. This is the Avicii drop — but instead of a synth lead, it's a human voice declaring gratitude into the dusk. Bars 15-16: instead of a full stop, the instruments begin their retreat — kick drops to half notes, strings sustain and begin to diminish, pad filter begins closing, creating a decrescendo bridge into the Bridge section. Dynamic level: ff at peak, beginning descent in final 2 bars.
**Bridge_8Bars:** The stripping bare — dramatic dynamic drop to pp. Everything falls away except the solo acoustic guitar returning to naked fingerpicking and the lead voice completely alone, no harmonies. The guitar plays the Dsus2→A/C#→F#m7→E progression with delicate, exposed fingerpicking — every finger squeak, every fret buzz is audible and welcome. The voice drops to its lowest, most vulnerable register — nearly speaking, letting cracks and catches show on 'One day I'll close my eyes.' This is the emotional climax through understatement. On 'And I'll tell the ones I love what I should've said then,' the voice wavers with real human emotion — a slight catch in the throat before 'love,' a micro-pause before 'said.' The vinyl crackle texture appears at subliminal volume, adding analog warmth to the emptiness. On 'I saw it — every bit of it —' a single sustained cello note (A2) fades in beneath, providing the barest harmonic foundation. 'Amen' is delivered at the threshold between singing and whispering, with earned vibrato that blooms and then releases. A single unison voice joins on 'amen' — same pitch (approximately D4), different timbre (imagine a woman's voice, a wife's voice, appearing beside the singer) — held for two beats, then both voices release into one full beat of absolute silence. No reverb tail. No pad. Just the room. Dynamic level: pp, with 'amen' approaching the threshold of audibility.
**Outro_12Bars:** The gentle fade — a slow, warm dissolution like golden hour becoming blue hour. Bars 1-4: guitar returns to gentle open strumming (not full chorus energy, more like a tired, grateful version), harmonies return in close two-part thirds on 'So let the good stay burning / Let the reel keep turning' — the two voices slightly offset in timing, one arriving a split-second after the other like an echo of memory. Synth pad returns as a distant, filtered wash. Kick returns as a single-note quarter-note pulse at low velocity. Strings play soft pizzicato arpeggios. Dynamic level: mp. Bars 5-8: 'We were golden, we were golden' in close two-part harmony, imperfect and warm. Banjo plays single tremolo notes, high and lonesome. Kick drops away. Strings switch from pizzicato to a single sustained chord that slowly diminishes. Pedal steel plays one long, weeping note. Dynamic level: p, diminishing. Bars 9-12 (ritardando): tempo gradually slows from 94 BPM to approximately 80 BPM over these final four bars. Guitar returns to solo fingerpicking, slowing with each bar. All other instruments have fallen away — bass gone, kick gone, pad fading, strings fading. The final two bars are guitar alone, each note placed with space around it. The last whispered 'And the good's still burning' is lead voice only — no harmony, no reverb, no processing. Just a human voice and the room and then silence. The guitar plays one final A chord, lets it ring, and the song ends on the natural decay of the strings. Dynamic level: p to pp to silence.

## Mixing Notes
VOCAL CHAIN: Large-diaphragm condenser (U87 character) positioned 8-10 inches from source, slight HPF at 80 Hz to remove proximity rumble, gentle 1176-style compression (4:1, slow attack 10ms to preserve transients, medium release) applying 3-4 dB of gain reduction in verses and 6-8 dB in choruses, followed by an LA-2A-style optical compressor for transparent leveling (2-3 dB GR). Subtle saturation (tape emulation) to add warmth and harmonic density to the vocal midrange. EQ: gentle scoop at 300-400 Hz to reduce muddiness, presence lift at 3-5 kHz (+2 dB shelf) for clarity and intimacy, air shelf at 12 kHz (+1.5 dB) for breathy top-end in verses only (roll off in choruses where the voice is fuller). De-ess conservatively — preserve sibilance character, only tame peaks above 7 kHz that become painful. REVERB: Two sends — (1) a short plate reverb (1.2s decay, pre-delay 30ms) for warmth and body, present throughout, mixed at -14dB under vocal; (2) a long hall reverb (3.5s decay, pre-delay 60ms) automated to swell in choruses and disappear in bridge/outro — gives the chorus its cathedral-in-a-field spaciousness. The final whispered line gets NO reverb — completely dry, shockingly intimate after the produced sections. HARMONY VOCALS: Similar chain but HPF at 120 Hz, more aggressive compression (6-8 dB GR) to sit them behind the lead, panned in a narrow spread (low third at 15% left, high fifth at 15% right) to create width without separation — these voices should feel like they emanate from the same body. Slight chorus effect (0.3ms modulation) on harmony bus to create ethereal shimmer. ACOUSTIC GUITAR: Recorded with two mics — small-diaphragm condenser (KM184 character) at 12th fret for detail and transient, large-diaphragm at bridge for body and low-end warmth. Blend 60/40 detail/body. Light compression (3:1, 2-3 dB GR). EQ: cut 200 Hz by -2 dB to prevent low-mid buildup with bass instruments, presence boost at 5 kHz for pick/finger clarity, high shelf at 10 kHz for air. Pan guitar 10% left of center to leave room for banjo (10% right). In verses, guitar is the widest element in the stereo field; in choruses, it narrows to center-left as other elements fill the panorama. BANJO: Bright and present but mixed 6-8 dB below guitar, panned 10-20% right. HPF at 150 Hz — banjo has no business in the low end. Light compression. The banjo should feel like a color accent, not a lead instrument. KICK: Layered — the organic kick (captured or sampled from a Nashville session) provides the attack and mid-body (80-120 Hz), the 808-style sub-kick provides the chest-punch low end (40-60 Hz). Blend so the attack is felt at 3-4 kHz as a gentle beater click and the body is felt in the solar plexus. Sidechain the synth pad and sub-bass to the kick with a fast attack (0.5ms) and medium release (150ms) — this creates the Avicii-style pumping effect that gives the choruses their rhythmic breath without ever feeling jarring. SNARE/CLAP: Organic brush snare panned dead center, layered with a soft electronic clap for width (clap slightly louder in Chorus 2). Reverb: short room (0.5s) to keep it tight. HI-HAT: Panned 20% right, filtered with a gentle LPF at 12 kHz to prevent harshness, velocity-mapped for human feel. SYNTH PAD: Warm analog character, recorded in stereo or with stereo chorus. Automated low-pass filter is the primary expressive tool — closed at 800 Hz in verses (felt but not heard), sweeping to 8 kHz through pre-choruses, fully open in choruses. Sidechain compression to kick is the signature production move — 4-6 dB of gain reduction pumping on quarter notes in choruses. Reverb: long tail (4s) with heavy pre-delay (80ms) to keep the pad spacious but behind the vocal. SUB-BASS: Mono, dead center, HPF at 25 Hz, LPF at 100 Hz — this is a pure low-frequency foundation. Sidechain to kick to prevent frequency-masking. ELECTRIC BASS: Warm, round P-bass tone. Compression (6:1) for evenness. EQ: boost at 700 Hz for midrange growl, cut at 250 Hz to prevent mud. Panned dead center. Mixed to sit just above the sub-bass and just below the guitar in frequency priority. STRINGS: Recorded or programmed with realistic articulation (legato in choruses, pizzicato in outro). Panned wide (violins 40% L/R, viola 20% L, cello 20% R) for cinematic stereo field. Light compression, generous hall reverb (3s) to place them in a virtual concert space behind the vocal. In Chorus 2, the string level comes up 3 dB and the reverb widens — the strings should feel like they suddenly fill the room. PEDAL STEEL: Drenched in plate reverb (2.5s), panned 25% left, mixed low (-10 dB below vocal) — this is a texture, not a feature. TAMBOURINE: Panned 30% right, HPF at 500 Hz, gentle compression, short room reverb. Should add rhythmic shimmer without drawing attention. ATMOSPHERIC TEXTURES: Field recording and vinyl crackle are utility layers — mixed at -28 to -35 dB, never consciously audible, only noticed if removed. They provide subliminal context and warmth. MASTER BUS: Gentle SSL-style bus compression (2:1, slow attack 30ms, auto release) applying 1-2 dB of glue compression. Subtle stereo widening on the sides above 5 kHz. Soft-clip limiter at -0.3 dBTP with target loudness of -11 LUFS integrated — this song needs dynamic range to breathe. The difference between the Bridge (pp) and Chorus 2 (ff) should be at least 8-10 dB in the final master. Do not crush the dynamics — the emotional arc IS the dynamics. STEREO FIELD PHILOSOPHY: Verses are narrow and intimate (guitar and voice clustered in the center 30% of the stereo field). Pre-choruses begin to widen as elements enter at wider pan positions. Choruses are panoramic — elements spread across the full stereo field, with the vocal anchored dead center as the sun around which everything orbits. Bridge collapses back to mono-center (voice and guitar only). Outro gradually narrows from wide to center, mirroring the visual metaphor of light concentrating to a single point before disappearing.

## Tempo & Feel
94 BPM with a laid-back, behind-the-beat quarter-note pulse — the groove sits approximately 15-20ms behind a rigid grid, giving it the unhurried porch-swing feel of a warm evening where nobody needs to be anywhere. The kick and bass lock together on a relaxed pocket that breathes rather than drives. Hi-hats have a subtle swing (approximately 58% ratio) that nods to both bluegrass shuffle and soulful head-bob. The ritardando in the final 4 bars of the Outro decelerates gradually from 94 to approximately 78-80 BPM, as if the song's heartbeat is slowing into sleep. Throughout, the tempo should feel human — no click track rigidity, micro-fluctuations of ±2 BPM are welcome and encouraged, particularly in the Bridge where the guitar should breathe with the singer's phrasing rather than a metronome.
